# E-E-A-T Scoring Checklist

E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) is Google's framework for evaluating content quality. Trust is the most important pillar. E-E-A-T is especially critical for YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) topics.

## The Four Pillars

### Experience (First-Hand)
- [ ] Content demonstrates the creator has actually done/used/visited what they write about
- [ ] Includes personal anecdotes, real-world examples, or case studies from direct experience
- [ ] Product reviews show evidence of actual use (original photos, screenshots, specific details)
- [ ] Travel/location content includes specific details only a visitor would know

### Expertise (Knowledge)
- [ ] Content is written or reviewed by a subject matter expert or demonstrable enthusiast
- [ ] Technical accuracy is verifiable and claims are well-supported
- [ ] Content depth goes beyond surface-level — covers nuances, edge cases, and practical details
- [ ] Author demonstrates specialized knowledge appropriate to the topic's complexity

### Authoritativeness (Recognition)
- [ ] Site is recognized as a go-to source in its field
- [ ] Content is cited or referenced by other reputable sources
- [ ] Brand has presence on authoritative platforms (industry publications, conferences, awards)
- [ ] Domain has earned quality backlinks from relevant, trusted sites

### Trustworthiness (Reliability)
- [ ] Site is served over HTTPS with valid SSL certificate
- [ ] Contact information is clearly accessible
- [ ] Privacy policy and terms of service are present and current
- [ ] No factual errors or misleading claims
- [ ] Transparent about sponsorships, affiliations, and potential conflicts of interest

## Google's "Who, How, Why" Framework

### Who Created the Content?
- [ ] Clear bylines on all informational pages
- [ ] Bylines link to detailed author bio pages
- [ ] Author pages include credentials, expertise areas, and professional background
- [ ] Multiple authors are individually credited where applicable

### How Was the Content Created?
- [ ] Methodology is transparent (how products were tested, data was collected, etc.)
- [ ] AI/automation usage is disclosed when applicable
- [ ] Review content details testing process with quantitative measurements
- [ ] Both benefits and drawbacks are discussed (not one-sided)

### Why Was the Content Created?
- [ ] Primary purpose is to help users, not manipulate rankings
- [ ] Content addresses genuine user needs and questions
- [ ] Not created solely to attract search engine traffic
- [ ] Provides actionable value beyond what competitors offer

## Site-Level E-E-A-T Audit Checks

### Trust Markers
- [ ] Robust "About Us" page explaining site background and why it should be trusted
- [ ] Clear editorial standards or content policy page
- [ ] Physical address or business registration details (where applicable)
- [ ] Customer testimonials or case studies with verifiable details
- [ ] Industry certifications, licenses, or regulatory compliance displayed

### Author Infrastructure
- [ ] Every content piece has a named author (not "Admin" or "Staff")
- [ ] Author bio pages exist with photo, credentials, social profiles, and published work
- [ ] Author schema markup (Person) is implemented
- [ ] Authors have verifiable external presence (LinkedIn, industry publications, speaking engagements)

### Content Quality Signals
- [ ] Citations and references to reputable sources (studies, reports, expert quotes)
- [ ] Original data, research, or unique insights (not just rewritten competitor content)
- [ ] Content displays accurate "Published on" or "Last updated" dates
- [ ] Visual evidence: original screenshots, real-world photos (not stock images)
- [ ] Methodology transparency for reviews and comparisons
- [ ] Content is regularly audited and updated for accuracy

### Technical Trust Signals
- [ ] HTTPS everywhere (no mixed content)
- [ ] No intrusive interstitials or deceptive ads
- [ ] Fast loading (Core Web Vitals passing)
- [ ] Mobile-friendly and accessible
- [ ] No security warnings or Safe Browsing issues

## Scoring Rubric

Each check item scores 0-2 points:
- **0** = Missing or critically deficient
- **1** = Partially implemented or needs improvement
- **2** = Fully implemented and high quality

### Score Interpretation
| Score Range | Grade | Assessment |
|-------------|-------|------------|
| 90-100% | A | Excellent E-E-A-T — likely trusted by Google |
| 75-89% | B | Good foundation — targeted improvements needed |
| 60-74% | C | Significant gaps — prioritize trust markers and author infrastructure |
| 40-59% | D | Weak E-E-A-T — major overhaul needed before expecting ranking improvement |
| 0-39% | F | Critical — site likely penalized or suppressed by helpful content system |

## YMYL Topic Heightened Standards

For Your Money or Your Life topics (health, finance, safety, legal, news), Google applies stricter E-E-A-T evaluation:
- Author MUST have verifiable professional credentials in the field
- Claims MUST be supported by authoritative sources (medical journals, regulatory bodies, etc.)
- Content MUST be reviewed by qualified professionals
- Outdated information is particularly harmful — update frequency matters more
- Disclaimers and professional advice notices should be present
